Different types of Painting Mediums

Welcome to the world of painting.

First, everyone is an artist! You are capable of producing quality works of art. The only thing you need are the tools and resources to get started. Below, you will find basic information about the types of paints to get started! By painting, you are allowing yourself to escape to a world where you don't have to limit your creativity and imagination!

Acrylics

Acrylics are great for first timers.

Pros:

  1. They dry quickly.Great if you are ready to move on with painting other things!
  2. They are cost-efficient
Cons:
  1. They dry quickly.Not great if you want to blend your colors
  2. Hard to get off of clothes

Oils

Oils are great if you are more experienced.

Pros:

  1. A little goes a long way!
  2. Blends with other paints beautifully!
Cons:
  1. Blends with other paints. Not a great way to find out the area you thought was dry, was in fact not.
  2. Noxious fumes, paint in an airy, open setting

Pastels

Pastels provide a soft touch to a portrait!

Pros:

  1. soft, vibrant colors!
  2. Minimal supplies needed to get started
Cons:
  1. Not ideal if you are prone to making a lot of mistakes
  2. Prone to smearing

Watercolors

Watercolors provide soft, flowy artwork

Pros:

  1. Waterbased! Very easy to work with and clean up
  2. The most affordable of all the painting mediums
Cons:
  1. Do not use if you hate making mistakes. Very hard to correct
  2. Difficult to layer unless the paint is completely dry.

Try it yourself

You now have the necessary knowledge to go forward from here.

Do not be afraid to try new painting mediums! It is a good idea to try

as many mediums as you can. Enjoy painting!
